# Observability System

## 1. Purpose
The Observability System provides real-time visibility into platform operations, user activity, and system health. It ensures:
- Rapid detection of issues and anomalies
- Data-driven decision making
- Continuous improvement and transparency

---

## 2. Core Principles

### 2.1 Comprehensive Coverage
- All critical systems and user flows are instrumented
- No blind spots in monitoring

### 2.2 Real-Time Monitoring
- Metrics, logs, and traces are collected and analyzed in real time
- Automated alerting for anomalies and failures

### 2.3 Actionable Insights
- Dashboards and reports drive operational and strategic decisions
- Observability data is accessible to relevant stakeholders

### 2.4 Privacy & Security
- Observability data is protected and access-controlled
- No sensitive user data is exposed in logs or metrics

---

## 3. Instrumentation Model
- Metrics: Quantitative measures of system and user activity
- Logs: Detailed records of events and errors
- Traces: End-to-end tracking of requests and flows

---

## 4. Alerting & Response
- Automated alerts for:
  - System failures
  - Security incidents
  - Exploit indicators
- Escalation protocols for critical issues

---

## 5. Reporting & Auditing
- Regular reports on system health, incidents, and trends
- Auditable records of all observability data and actions

---

## 6. Open Decisions
- Metric and log retention policy
- Access control for observability data
- Dashboard/report expansion strategy

---

## 7. Summary
The Observability System ensures:
- The platform is transparent, reliable, and continuously improving
- Issues are detected and resolved rapidly
- All observability data is secure, actionable, and privacy-respecting
